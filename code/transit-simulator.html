Transit Simulator

I saw an awesome simulated map of the Portland, OR transit system. It was written in the language R, so I was inspired to write a similar version in Python, a language I was more familiar with. My script is able to take any transit agency's GTFS file, a standardized format for transit timetables, and visualize the routes at any day or time. This project is an intersection of my passion for transit and my personal desire to work on coding projects.

Federal Way Link Extension - Seattle, WA

In December, 2025, three new stations were added to the 1 Line, shown here in green.

E, F, M, R Trains - New York, NY

This simulation shows the busy lines that serve three of New York City's five boroughs; Brooklyn, Manhattan, and Queens.


Here, the vehicle nodes are colored based on their line to help distinguish transit lines that share the same track.
